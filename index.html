What I do

Three things.
All of them matter.

Every engagement starts with understanding your team and your business. Then I get to work on the things that actually move the needle.

01

Build a winning culture

Sales teams don't underperform because of talent. They underperform because the culture isn't right. I build the environment where sellers want to show up, compete, and win — and where that culture outlasts my engagement.

02

Develop individuals

I've had 20+ sellers go on to VP and head of sales roles. That doesn't happen by accident. I coach people on how to sell, how to communicate, and how to grow — using emotional intelligence frameworks that most managers never touch.

03

Align teams internally

Sales doesn't exist in isolation. When sales, marketing, product, and leadership aren't communicating — the sellers feel it and buyers feel it. I fix the internal alignment that makes everything else work better.

How it works

I come in, fix it,
and leave you better.

Week one — I listen first

Owner intake, individual seller conversations, call reviews, pipeline assessment. I understand what's actually happening before I say anything about what to do.

The engagement — daily presence

Daily seller check-ins. Bi-weekly 1:1 coaching. Pipeline reviews. Weekly plain-language summary to you. I'm embedded in your team's rhythm — not just showing up for a monthly call.

The handoff — you keep what we built

The culture, the process, the accountability systems, the onboarding framework — all of it stays with you. The team keeps performing because the foundation is solid, not because I'm still there.

AI-assisted throughout

Call pattern analysis, behavioral tracking, pipeline synthesis — AI makes the coaching more precise and the reporting more accurate. You get real data, not rep self-reporting.

Most consultants come in, tell you what's wrong, and hand you a deck. I come in as your sales manager — showing up every day, coaching your people, and building something that runs without me. That's the work most consultants don't want to do. It's the only work that actually sticks.
